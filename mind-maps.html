How to Use These Maps

For Quick Orientation

When you need to understand a topic quickly, start with the relevant mind map to grasp the key concepts and relationships. Then drill into the detailed documentation pages for deeper understanding.

For Presentations

These mind maps work well as presentation backgrounds or reference materials when explaining concepts to colleagues or customers. They provide visual structure to verbal explanations.

For Documentation

Mind maps serve as visual outlines for documentation. When writing detailed content, use the mind map structure as your outline to ensure comprehensive coverage.

For Strategic Discussions

Use the Master Operating System and Strategic maps when discussing product direction, portfolio allocation, or organizational strategy. They help align teams around shared frameworks.

Printing & Sharing

These mind maps are designed to be:

  • Printable: Print at full size for office walls or bring to meetings
  • Shareable: Download and share in presentations or documentation
  • Memorable: Visual representations help with retention and recall
  • Discussion Starters: Use as conversation prompts in team meetings

Learning Path

For new hires, we recommend this learning sequence:

  1. Day 1: Review Product Operating System Master Map for context
  2. Week 1: Study PDLC Framework, Capital Allocation, and Competitive Battlefield
  3. Week 2: Review Financial Engine and Day 1 Battle Plan
  4. Week 3: Understand Operating Cadence and Product Portfolio
  5. Week 4: Review M&A Timeline and explore detailed pages for deeper learning

Staying Updated

Mind maps are updated quarterly as strategy evolves. Check back regularly for updates or ask your manager for the latest versions.
