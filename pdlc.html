PDLC Framework

7-phase product development lifecycle with stage gates and scalable rigor across four tiers.

Quick Summary

The PDLC is a 7-phase, stage-gated framework (Discovery → Definition & Business Case → Design → Development → Validation & QA → Launch & GTM → Optimization) with 4 tiers of scalable rigor. Tier 1 strategic initiatives (>$500K investment, >$1M ARR) run the full framework. Tier 4 expedited fixes ship in days via Kanban. Every phase ends with a decision: GO, NO-GO, PIVOT, or PARK. Governance is owned by named decision-makers with clear approval rights by tier.

The Seven Phases

1. Discovery

Transform market signals, client feedback, and competitive intelligence into validated product opportunities. Key activities include market opportunity sizing vs. Lyric, Machinify, and Cotiviti, client advisory board input, regulatory landscape assessment, and technology feasibility with the CTO org. Gate: Opportunity brief approved with TAM estimate, competitive differentiation thesis, and preliminary technical feasibility. Timeline: 2–4 weeks.

2. Definition & Business Case

Develop detailed requirements, financial projections, and resource plans to secure executive approval. Includes BRD & financial model construction, PRD development, regulatory/compliance impact assessment with Legal, architecture review with the CTO organization, and cross-functional alignment with Sales. Gate: Business case approved by the President (Jim Bridges) with committed resources, regulatory sign-off, and technical architecture endorsed. Timeline: 3–6 weeks.

3. Design

Translate validated requirements into detailed technical designs, UX specifications, and architecture plans. Includes technical architecture design, UX/UI wireframes, API contract definitions, data model design, and security/compliance design review. Gate: Technical design document approved, UX designs validated, architecture review completed. Timeline: 2–4 weeks.

4. Development

Execute technical development with continuous integration, testing, and compliance validation. Agile sprints with bi-weekly demos, CI/CD pipeline adherence, HIPAA security controls, and India GCC coordination for appropriate workstreams. Gate: Feature complete with >85% test coverage, security review completed, performance benchmarks met. Timeline: 6–16 weeks.

5. Validation & Quality Assurance

Comprehensive validation in production-like environments with client pilot programs. UAT with internal stakeholders and beta clients, performance/load testing, client pilot program (2–3 Tier 2/3 clients), and claims accuracy validation using historical data comparison. Gate: UAT sign-off, pilot success metrics achieved, go-live checklist complete. Timeline: 4–8 weeks.

6. Launch & GTM

Coordinate market launch across Sales, Marketing, Implementation, and Client Success. Sales enablement (battle cards, pricing guidance), marketing collateral, implementation playbook updates, client expansion campaigns, and analyst briefings (KLAS, Everest Group, Gartner). Gate: All GTM materials approved, Sales team trained, first deals in pipeline, support teams ready. Timeline: 2–4 weeks.

7. Optimization

Monitor post-launch performance, capture learnings, and prioritize enhancements. KPI monitoring dashboard (adoption rates, accuracy metrics, client satisfaction), win/loss analysis feeding back to Discovery, quarterly business reviews with pilot clients, and continuous improvement backlog refinement. Gate: 30/60/90-day post-launch reviews completed with documented learnings and prioritized enhancement roadmap. Timeline: Ongoing.

Governance & Decision Rights

Each tier has defined decision-makers and approval requirements:

  • Tier 1 — Strategic Initiative (>$500K investment, >$1M ARR): President + Board approval, full 7-phase PDLC with all gates. Timeline: 6–12 months.
  • Tier 2 — Major Enhancement ($100K–$500K, $250K–$1M ARR): President approval at Phase 2, full PDLC with streamlined Discovery. Timeline: 3–6 months.
  • Tier 3 — Standard Feature (<$100K, <$250K ARR): Product Director approval, expedited 3-phase (Define, Build, Launch). Timeline: 4–10 weeks.
  • Tier 4 — Expedited Fix (Minimal investment): Product Manager approval, Kanban workflow. Timeline: 1–3 weeks.

Key Success Factors

  • Clear Decision Criteria: GO/NO-GO/PIVOT/PARK decisions are objective and data-driven
  • Cross-functional Alignment: Engineering and Product partnership at every phase
  • Scalable Process: The framework adapts to initiative size and complexity
  • Documented Rationale: Every decision is documented for future reference and learning
  • Regular Syncs: Weekly product syncs keep teams aligned on progress and blockers

Getting Started

New initiatives begin by identifying their tier and investment requirements. Use the PDLC Stage Gate Tracker to monitor progress. Reference the PRD Template and Discovery Framework for phase-specific deliverables. Attend the Product Planning Cadence meetings to understand SAFe integration.
