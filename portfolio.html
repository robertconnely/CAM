Product Portfolio

M&A history, product line overview, and portfolio evolution.

Quick Summary

Zelis's product portfolio is built through strategic acquisitions dating back to its formation. The M&A timeline shows how capabilities in Payment Integrity, OON Pricing, and Provider solutions were assembled through targeted acquisitions. Understanding this history is critical for navigating the current technology landscape and identifying integration opportunities.

Current Product Lines

Payment Integrity (PI)

Purpose: Pre-payment claims editing and post-payment recovery to identify and prevent claim overpayments.

Key Capabilities:

  • Pre-payment claim editing with 1000+ clinical and billing rules
  • DRG assignment and validation using GROUPER algorithms
  • Unbundling detection and downcoding prevention
  • Fraud/Waste/Abuse (FWA) detection using predictive analytics
  • Post-payment recovery for identified overpayments
  • Savings tracking and reporting

Core Technology: ZIPP (Zelis Intelligent Processing Platform)

Market Position: Third largest player by market share after Cotiviti and Lyric. Fastest growing and highest NPS of competitors. Modern technology platform vs. legacy solutions.

OON Pricing

Purpose: Manage out-of-network claim costs through repricing and negotiation.

Key Capabilities:

  • OON claim identification and routing
  • Repricing against in-network benchmarks or custom rates
  • Reference-Based Pricing (RBP) implementation
  • Provider negotiation support with data analytics
  • Member communication and balance bill prevention
  • Appeals and dispute management

Market Position: Strong position with ~81% of repriced claims. Competing against Lyric (market leader), Machinify (emerging), Cotiviti (smaller OON presence).

Provider Solutions

Purpose: Help providers understand and manage payer claim decisions.

Key Capabilities:

  • Provider access to payer claim data and decision rationales
  • Denial analysis and appeal tools
  • Coding improvement analytics
  • Financial performance benchmarking

Market Position: Emerging opportunity. Relationships through network business create unique entry point.

M&A Timeline & Acquisition History

Company Formation & Early Growth

Zelis was founded as a healthcare analytics and claims processing company. Early capabilities in claims data processing and provider networks formed the foundation.

Payment Integrity Capabilities Build (2015-2018)

Strategic acquisitions added depth to the Payment Integrity product line. Key additions:

  • Clinical validation and DRG assignment technology
  • Fraud detection and predictive analytics
  • Rules engine and decision logic capabilities
  • Customer relationships in the PI market

OON Pricing Market Entry (2018-2020)

Acquisition of repricing and RBP capabilities allowed Zelis to enter the growing OON market:

  • Repricing technology and benchmarking data
  • Reference-Based Pricing expertise
  • Customer relationships with large national payers
  • Provider negotiation tools and analytics

Platform Integration & Modernization (2020-Present)

Moving toward unified technology platform and integrated customer solutions:

  • Cloud-native architecture and modern tech stack
  • API-first integration approach
  • AI/ML capabilities for next-generation optimization
  • Combined PI+OON offering for greater customer value

Customer Segments & Use Cases

Commercial Payers

Large and mid-market health insurance companies are the primary Zelis customers.

Use Cases:

  • Medical Loss Ratio (MLR) reduction and profitability improvement
  • Claims accuracy and fraud prevention
  • OON cost management and rate negotiation
  • Compliance with regulatory requirements

Regional Blues Plans

Smaller regional health insurance plans with limited in-house analytics capabilities.

Use Cases:

  • Contract-based PI and OON solutions
  • Outsourced claims optimization services
  • Managed service offerings with shared savings models

Self-Insured Employers (Emerging)

Large employers managing their own health insurance plans through TPA relationships.

Use Cases:

  • OON cost management for employee health plans
  • Claims analytics and benchmarking
  • Direct TPA partnership models

Technology Architecture

ZIPP Platform (Zelis Intelligent Processing Platform)

The modern foundation powering both PI and OON processing:

  • Claims Ingest: Accepts claims from multiple EDI formats and data sources
  • Clinical Validation: Applies clinical rules and DRG assignment logic
  • PI Rules Engine: Pre-payment and post-payment editing rules
  • Pricing Engine: Repricing and RBP calculations
  • Decision & Output: Claim decisions with detailed rationale for appeals
  • Reporting & Analytics: Savings tracking, provider performance, trends

Legacy Systems (Phase-out Strategy)

Older acquisition-based systems are being consolidated into ZIPP as part of multi-year modernization:

  • PIConnect (legacy PI system) ‚Üí ZIPP consolidation
  • OON Legacy System ‚Üí ZIPP consolidation
  • Analytics Platform ‚Üí Unified data warehouse and analytics engine

Integration Opportunities & Challenges

Integration Opportunities

  • Data Consolidation: Combine clinical, network, and pricing data for better insights
  • Rule Optimization: PI and OON rules can inform each other for better outcomes
  • Customer Economics: Integrated solution commands higher ACV and gross margin
  • Technology Efficiency: Single platform reduces operational costs

Integration Challenges

  • Legacy System Complexity: Some legacy systems have deep customer dependencies
  • Data Quality: Consolidating data from multiple sources requires careful validation
  • Customer Migration: Moving customers to new platform requires careful planning
  • Competitive Risk: Extended migration period creates customer vulnerability

Portfolio Strategy 2026-2028

Product Line Evolution

  • PI 2.0: AI-native pre-payment and post-payment optimization
  • OON 2.0: Predictive repricing and provider negotiation
  • Integrated Platform: Single consolidated system for all claims processing
  • Adjacent Products: Risk adjustment, TPA solutions, provider analytics

Technology Roadmap

  • ZIPP becomes 100% of new customer deployments by 2026
  • Legacy system migrations complete by 2027
  • Cloud-native, AI-enabled unified platform by 2028
  • Real-time processing and decision-making capabilities

Competitive Product Comparison

Lyric (Competitive Benchmark)

  • Strengths: OON market dominance, large customer base, established brand
  • Weaknesses: Legacy technology, weak PI capabilities, slow innovation
  • Zelis Advantage: Modern platform, integrated PI+OON, AI innovation

Machinify (Growth Competitor)

  • Strengths: Modern tech stack, AI-first approach, transparent models
  • Weaknesses: Smaller customer base, limited market share, newer company
  • Zelis Advantage: Larger installed base, integrated platform, data scale

Cotiviti (PI Leader)

  • Strengths: Largest PI player by revenue, consultative approach
  • Weaknesses: Slower innovation, service-heavy model, integration challenges post-acquisition
  • Zelis Advantage: Technology-first approach, modern platform, integrated OON capability

Key Takeaways

  • Portfolio Strength: Zelis has a strong, complementary product portfolio covering PI and OON
  • M&A Integration: Successful acquisition integration is an ongoing priority
  • Technology Modernization: Platform consolidation is critical for future growth
  • Market Position: Strong competitive positioning with growth opportunity
  • Expansion Potential: Adjacent market opportunities could 2x revenue
