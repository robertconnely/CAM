Finance Strategy

Unit economics, ROIC value drivers, and revenue flow analysis.

Quick Summary

Zelis operates a transaction-based revenue model charging per-claim fees. Revenue flows through three tiers: access fees, per-claim processing, and performance-based savings shares. The ROIC value driver tree connects product decisions to enterprise financial outcomes. Unit economics vary by product line - Payment Integrity generates higher margins on pre-payment editing vs. post-payment recovery.

Revenue Model

Three-Tier Revenue Structure

Tier 1: Access Fees

Customers pay a fixed annual or monthly fee for platform access. This creates predictable recurring revenue and aligns with infrastructure costs. Access fees represent the foundation of the contractual relationship.

Tier 2: Per-Claim Processing

For each claim processed through the platform, Zelis charges a transaction fee. This fee varies by:

  • Product line (PI vs. OON)
  • Processing type (pre-payment editing vs. post-payment recovery)
  • Customer volume and negotiated rates
  • Data enrichment and analytics services included

Tier 3: Performance-Based Savings Shares

Zelis shares in a percentage of the savings it generates for customers. This aligns incentives and creates upside potential. Savings share percentages are typically:

  • Payment Integrity: 15-25% of savings delivered
  • OON Pricing: 10-20% of savings delivered
  • Negotiated higher for strategic customers with integrated solutions

Unit Economics

Payment Integrity Economics

Pre-Payment Editing:

  • Per-claim fee: $0.15-$0.35 depending on complexity
  • Gross margin: 70-80% (low cost of goods sold)
  • Average customer saves 0.5-2% of claims paid
  • Zelis share: 20-25% of savings delivered

Post-Payment Recovery:

  • Per-claim fee: $0.10-$0.20
  • Gross margin: 65-75%
  • Recovery rate: 30-50% of overpayments identified
  • Zelis share: 15-20% of recoveries

OON Pricing Economics

  • Per-claim fee: $0.20-$0.50 for repricing
  • Gross margin: 75-85%
  • Average customer saves 10-15% on OON claims
  • Zelis share: 10-20% of savings delivered
  • RBP (Reference Based Pricing): Negotiated annual contract with volume discounts

ROIC Value Driver Tree

The ROIC (Return on Invested Capital) value driver tree connects product decisions to enterprise financial outcomes:

Core Formula

ROIC = (Revenue - Operating Costs) / Invested Capital

Key Value Drivers

  • Revenue Growth: New customer acquisition, expansion revenue from existing customers, product mix shift toward higher-margin offerings
  • Gross Margin: Operating leverage from per-claim processing, SaaS platform scaling, reduction in cost of goods sold
  • Operating Efficiency: Automation and AI reducing manual review costs, platform consolidation reducing duplicate infrastructure, scaling team productivity
  • Capital Efficiency: Faster time to profitability for new initiatives, improved cash conversion, reduced working capital requirements

Financial Planning Principles

1. Align Product with Unit Economics

Every new initiative must have a clear path to positive unit economics. Use the unit economics model to stress-test financial assumptions before committing to development.

2. Track ROIC Metrics

Monitor realized ROIC for completed initiatives vs. projections. Use this data to improve capital allocation accuracy over time.

3. Optimize the Value Driver Tree

Focus product development on the highest-leverage value drivers:

  • Revenue expansion: Integrated PI+OON solution for higher customer spend
  • Gross margin: Automation and AI to reduce cost per claim
  • Operating efficiency: Platform consolidation and shared infrastructure

4. Scenario Planning

Financial projections include base case, upside, and downside scenarios. Product decisions should be robust across scenarios.

Key Financial Metrics

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): Annualized value of recurring contracts
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Sales and marketing spend per new customer
  • Payback Period: Months to recover CAC from contribution margin
  • Gross Margin: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue for each product line
  • Magic Number: Net new ARR / prior quarter sales and marketing spend (efficiency indicator)
  • Rule of 40: Growth rate + Profitability margin (40+ is excellent)

Integration with Capital Allocation

Finance metrics directly feed capital allocation decisions:

  • Initiatives with >25% projected IRR are considered for Tier 1-2 funding
  • Unit economics models determine customer lifetime value (LTV) and payback period
  • ROIC tracking validates financial assumptions and improves future forecasting accuracy
