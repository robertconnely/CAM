Capital Allocation

Investment scoring, IRR thresholds, and tier-based capital allocation framework.

Quick Summary

Capital allocation uses a multi-factor scoring model combining Revenue Impact, Competitive Positioning, Technical Complexity, and Strategic Alignment. Initiatives scoring above the threshold advance with President/Board approval for Tier 1-2. The IRR threshold is >25% for committed capital. Business cases require TAM sizing, competitive gap analysis, and implementation cost before President approval.

Scoring Model

The Capital Allocation model evaluates initiatives across four core dimensions:

1. Revenue Impact (40% weight)

  • Total addressable market (TAM) sizing
  • Projected annual recurring revenue (ARR) impact
  • Customer acquisition and expansion potential
  • Baseline: >$1M ARR impact for Tier 1 initiatives

2. Competitive Positioning (25% weight)

  • Differentiation vs. Lyric, Machinify, Cotiviti
  • Defensibility through technology or network effects
  • Market share opportunity
  • Time-to-market urgency

3. Technical Complexity (20% weight)

  • Development effort estimation
  • Integration with existing systems
  • Data requirements and availability
  • Risk factors and mitigation strategies

4. Strategic Alignment (15% weight)

  • Alignment with 2026-2028 vision pillars
  • Platform consolidation progress
  • AI-native capability enablement
  • Market expansion objectives

IRR Threshold & Investment Tiers

All committed capital must meet a minimum Internal Rate of Return (IRR) threshold of 25% over the investment period. Initiatives are classified by investment size:

  • Tier 1: >$500K investment - President & Board approval required
  • Tier 2: $100-500K investment - VP Product & Finance approval required
  • Tier 3: $25-100K investment - Director-level approval required
  • Tier 4: <$25K investment - Team lead approval

Business Case Requirements

Every Tier 1 and 2 initiative requires a comprehensive business case including:

  • Executive Summary: 1-page overview of the opportunity and investment ask
  • Market Opportunity: TAM, TAM sizing methodology, competitive gap analysis
  • Solution Overview: Product approach, differentiation, go-to-market strategy
  • Financial Projections: 3-year revenue model, cost of investment, IRR calculation
  • Implementation Plan: PDLC phasing, resource requirements, timeline
  • Risk Assessment: Market risk, technical risk, execution risk, mitigation strategies
  • Decision Criteria: GO/NO-GO decision points and exit criteria

Portfolio Governance

The Quarterly Business Review (QBR) is the primary governance forum for capital allocation decisions. Portfolio health is reviewed across:

  • Strategic initiatives (Tier 1-2) status and performance
  • Tactical initiatives (Tier 3-4) progress and resource utilization
  • Emerging opportunities and market conditions
  • Annual capital budget allocation and rebalancing

Key Metrics to Track

  • Portfolio ARR Impact: Total annual recurring revenue from invested initiatives
  • Capital Efficiency: ARR per dollar of development investment
  • Initiative Success Rate: Percentage of initiatives meeting revenue targets
  • Time to Revenue: Months from funding decision to first revenue recognition
  • Realized IRR: Actual IRR vs. projected at decision time
