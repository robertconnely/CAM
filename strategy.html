Product Strategy

2026-2028 vision, AI innovation prioritization, and market expansion.

Quick Summary

The 2026-2028 Product Vision centers on three strategic pillars: Platform Consolidation (unifying PI and OON into an integrated claims intelligence platform), AI-Native Capabilities (embedding ML across the claims lifecycle), and Market Expansion (extending into adjacent markets including TPA, risk adjustment, and provider-side analytics). The AI Innovation Prioritization Matrix scores opportunities across feasibility, market impact, competitive urgency, and data readiness.

Strategic Vision 2026-2028

Mission

Transform healthcare economics by making intelligent claims processing the foundation of payer profitability.

Vision Statement

Zelis will be the leading integrated claims intelligence platform, delivering superior outcomes through AI-native, real-time optimization across the entire claims lifecycle.

Three Strategic Pillars

1. Platform Consolidation

Unify Payment Integrity and OON Pricing into a single integrated platform where claims are processed once with all rule engines and analytics applied simultaneously.

Current State:

  • PI and OON operate on separate technology stacks
  • Customers must maintain separate integrations
  • Data is not shared between systems

2026-2028 Target:

  • Single claims ingest and processing pipeline
  • Unified data model spanning clinical, network, and pricing data
  • Rules engines execute in parallel, not sequentially
  • Real-time feedback loop from clinical validation to pricing logic

Customer Benefits:

  • 25-40% higher savings through integrated optimization
  • Single integration point for IT teams
  • Unified reporting across both product lines
  • Improved data quality through integrated validation

2. AI-Native Capabilities

Embed machine learning throughout the claims lifecycle to automate manual processes, improve accuracy, and deliver better outcomes.

AI Innovation Areas:

  • Claims Classification: ML-based claim categorization for smart routing and processing
  • Provider Targeting: Predictive analytics to identify high-value optimization opportunities
  • Outcome Prediction: Models predicting claim outcome and savings potential
  • Anomaly Detection: Real-time fraud and waste detection
  • Natural Language Processing: Extract clinically relevant information from unstructured data
  • Explainable AI: Transparent models with clear reasoning for claims decisions

Investment Approach:

  • Tier 1 initiatives with >$1M ARR potential
  • Hybrid build/partner model (build core, partner on specialized AI)
  • Data strategy to support training and continuous improvement
  • Transparent model design for healthcare regulatory compliance

3. Market Expansion

Extend Zelis beyond payer-side claims optimization into adjacent markets where claims intelligence creates value.

Market Expansion Opportunities:

  • Third-Party Administrators (TPAs): Help TPAs manage claims for their self-insured customers
  • Risk Adjustment: Leverage clinical data to improve risk adjustment accuracy and revenue
  • Provider Analytics: Help providers understand payer claim denials and improve coding
  • Employer Direct: Self-insured employers managing their own claims
  • Government Programs: Medicaid and Medicare data processing and optimization

Entry Strategy:

  • Pilots with 3-5 customers in each market by end of 2026
  • Product enhancements based on pilot learnings
  • GTM partnerships with consulting and implementation firms
  • Acquisition targets to accelerate capability building (e.g., risk adjustment expertise)

AI Innovation Prioritization

The AI Innovation Prioritization Matrix evaluates opportunities across four dimensions:

Scoring Dimensions

  • Feasibility (25%): Can we build this with our current data and ML capabilities?
  • Market Impact (30%): What is the revenue upside and competitive advantage?
  • Competitive Urgency (25%): Are competitors moving into this space? Time-to-market advantage?
  • Data Readiness (20%): Do we have quality training data to support model development?

High-Priority AI Initiatives (Score >75/100)

  • Claims outcome prediction for pre-payment optimization
  • Provider targeting for outreach and negotiation
  • Real-time anomaly detection for fraud prevention
  • Natural language processing for clinical documentation

Competitive Positioning

Against Lyric, Machinify, and Cotiviti, Zelis differentiates through:

  • Integrated Platform: Only vendor with unified PI + OON solution
  • AI Leadership: First-mover advantage in claims outcome prediction
  • Data Advantage: Largest proprietary dataset spanning clinical, network, and claims
  • Modern Tech Stack: Cloud-native architecture vs. legacy systems
  • Provider Relationships: Unique position through provider network relationships

Success Metrics

  • Revenue: 3-year ARR target (to be set at annual planning)
  • Platform Adoption: % of new customers on consolidated platform
  • AI Productivity: Claims processed with AI models / total claims
  • Market Share: % market share in PI and OON vs. competitors
  • IPO Readiness: Board metrics and enterprise metrics (see Finance Strategy)
