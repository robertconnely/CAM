PRD Template

The standardized PRD (Product Requirements Document) template ensures consistency and completeness across all product initiatives:

PRD Sections

  • Executive Summary: 1-page overview of the problem, solution, and business impact
  • Problem Statement: Customer pain point and market opportunity
  • Solution Overview: How the product will solve the problem
  • Target Customers: User personas and segment focus
  • Success Metrics: KPIs that define success
  • Use Cases: Detailed user stories and workflows
  • Acceptance Criteria: Testable conditions for product completion
  • Non-Functional Requirements: Performance, security, scalability
  • Go-to-Market Plan: Launch timeline, sales approach, customer communication
  • Financial Model: Revenue projections, unit economics, payback period
  • Risks & Mitigation: Key risks and contingency plans

Meeting Agenda Templates

Standardized agenda templates ensure meetings stay focused and productive:

Weekly Product Sync Agenda (1 hour)

  • Opening remarks: 5 min
  • Pipeline status roundtable: 15 min
  • Initiative blockers and escalations: 20 min
  • Cross-functional coordination: 10 min
  • Closing and action items: 10 min

Monthly Roadmap Review Agenda (2 hours)

  • Market update: 15 min
  • Competitive moves and insights: 15 min
  • Roadmap recommendations for Q+1: 30 min
  • Customer feedback and win/loss analysis: 20 min
  • Sales pipeline impact and revenue forecast: 20 min
  • Decisions and next steps: 20 min

Quarterly Business Review Agenda (4 hours)

  • Opening remarks and objectives: 15 min
  • Q3 execution summary: 30 min
  • Financial results and metrics: 30 min
  • Strategic initiatives status: 45 min
  • Competitive analysis and market positioning: 30 min
  • Q4 roadmap and capital allocation: 45 min
  • Risk assessment and board readiness: 30 min
  • Closing and action items: 15 min

Competitive Battle Card Template

Sales-ready battle cards provide quick reference for competitive positioning:

Battle Card Sections

  • Company Overview: Founded, funding, employees, headquarters
  • Product Portfolio: Core products, target markets, pricing model
  • Zelis vs. [Competitor]: Feature comparison matrix
  • Win Themes: Top 3-5 reasons to choose Zelis
  • Loss Themes: Common competitor objections and rebuttals
  • Customer References: Customers using both products (if any)
  • Talking Points: Differentiators and proof points
  • Discount Guidelines: Approval thresholds for pricing negotiations

Jira & Confluence Implementation

The Confluence/Jira Implementation Guide documents how PDLC phases map to Jira workflows and Confluence documentation:

Jira Workflow Mapping

  • Discovery: Epic created, research tasks opened, story refinement
  • Definition: PRD finalized, story point estimation, sprint planning
  • Design: Design review tickets, architecture ADRs, technical specification
  • Development: Sprint execution, daily standups, code review
  • Validation: Test execution, defect resolution, UAT coordination
  • Launch: Go-live checklist, production monitoring, postmortem planning
  • Optimize: Backlog refinement, performance monitoring, roadmap updates

Confluence Documentation Standards

  • Product Requirements: PRD published in Confluence with embedded Jira links
  • Architecture Decisions: ADRs (Architecture Decision Records) document technical choices
  • Launch Playbook: Go-live checklist and runbook for launches
  • Postmortem Documentation: Root cause analysis and lessons learned
  • Customer References: Case studies and customer success stories

How to Use These Templates

For New Initiatives

  1. Download the PRD Template and populate sections based on your opportunity
  2. Present to Product Director for feedback and refinement
  3. Once approved, create Jira Epic and link to Confluence PRD page
  4. Use Meeting Agenda Templates for roadmap review presentation

For Competitive Situations

  1. Download the Battle Card Template for the relevant competitor
  2. Work with Sales for customer feedback and win/loss data
  3. Finalize and distribute to sales team before customer meetings
  4. Update quarterly based on competitive moves and market feedback

For Daily Execution

  1. Use Meeting Agenda Templates for each operating cadence meeting
  2. Link PRDs to Jira Epics for traceability
  3. Document architecture decisions in Confluence ADRs
  4. Track progress against PRD acceptance criteria in Jira

Best Practices

  • Keep Templates Current: Update templates quarterly based on lessons learned
  • Customize for Complexity: Use full template for Tier 1, simplified version for Tier 4
  • Link Everything: Cross-link Confluence, Jira, and calendar for visibility
  • Version Control: Track changes to templates and document rationale
  • Training: New team members should review completed examples during onboarding
