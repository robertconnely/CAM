Operating Cadence

Five rhythms of review creating accountability without bureaucracy.

Quick Summary

Five rhythms of review create accountability without bureaucracy. Weekly Product Sync (SVP + Product Directors): pipeline, blockers, resources. Bi-weekly Tech Alignment (SVP + CTO): architecture, capacity. Monthly Roadmap Review (President + leadership): strategic alignment. Quarterly Business Review (Executive Steering): portfolio, market positioning, IPO metrics. Annual Strategic Planning: corporate objectives, board commitments.

The Five Rhythms

1. Weekly Product Sync (1 hour)

Cadence: Every Monday, 2 PM

Attendees: SVP Product, Product Directors (PI, OON, Operations)

Purpose: Synchronize product execution, resolve blockers, align on weekly priorities

Agenda:

  • Pipeline status (new opportunities, deals in motion)
  • Initiative progress (PDLC phase updates, delivery confidence)
  • Blocker escalation (resource constraints, technical risks)
  • Cross-functional coordination (engineering, sales, customer success)
  • Quick wins and celebrations

2. Bi-weekly Tech Alignment (1.5 hours)

Cadence: Every other Wednesday, 10 AM

Attendees: SVP Product, CTO, Principal Engineers, Technical Architects

Purpose: Ensure technology roadmap alignment with product strategy

Agenda:

  • Architecture reviews for major initiatives
  • Technical debt assessment and prioritization
  • Capacity planning and resource allocation
  • Technology risk assessment
  • Innovation opportunity discussion

3. Monthly Roadmap Review (2 hours)

Cadence: First Wednesday of the month, 3 PM

Attendees: President, CFO, SVP Product, Product Directors, CTO, VP Sales

Purpose: Align product roadmap with business strategy and market conditions

Agenda:

  • Market update (competitive moves, customer feedback, regulatory changes)
  • Roadmap adjustments for upcoming quarter
  • Capital allocation opportunities and re-balancing
  • Customer win/loss analysis and insights
  • Sales pipeline and revenue forecast impact

4. Quarterly Business Review (QBR) (4 hours)

Cadence: End of each quarter (January, April, July, October)

Attendees: Executive Leadership, Board members (as appropriate), cross-functional teams

Purpose: Assess portfolio performance, strategic progress, and re-prioritize

Agenda:

  • Q3 execution summary (initiatives completed, revenue impact)
  • Q4 roadmap and capital allocation
  • Company metrics and KPI review
  • Market analysis and competitive positioning
  • Strategic initiatives status and updated projections
  • Board metrics and IPO readiness assessment
  • Risk assessment and mitigation strategies

5. Annual Strategic Planning (2-3 days offsite)

Cadence: October - November (planning for next calendar year)

Attendees: All executives and key managers

Purpose: Define annual corporate objectives, product vision, and capital plans

Agenda:

  • Market analysis and competitive positioning
  • Corporate financial targets and IPO objectives
  • Product vision and strategic pillars for the upcoming 3 years
  • Capital allocation framework for the year
  • Organizational structure and talent planning
  • Risk assessment and board-level briefing preparation

Meeting Discipline

Effective operating cadence requires disciplined execution:

Before Each Meeting

  • Agenda is published 24 hours in advance
  • All supporting materials are available (no surprises)
  • Owners are held accountable for prepared updates

During Each Meeting

  • Start on time, end on time - respect everyone's calendar
  • Decisions are made and captured
  • Action items are assigned with owners and due dates
  • Escalations are resolved in real-time when possible

After Each Meeting

  • Minutes are published within 24 hours
  • Action items are tracked in a shared system
  • Status is reported in the next meeting

Key Metrics Tracked

Operating cadence meetings track these key metrics:

  • Initiative Health: On-track, at-risk, blocked status for all Tier 1-2 initiatives
  • Revenue Pipeline: Deal stage, expected close date, probability-weighted ARR
  • Customer Satisfaction: NPS, customer health scores, churn indicators
  • Financial Performance: Revenue vs. plan, unit economics, customer acquisition cost (CAC)
  • Team Capacity: Utilization, open headcount, recruitment pipeline
  • Product Velocity: Delivery rate, quality metrics, technical debt trends

Getting Involved

New team members should:

  • Schedule 1:1s with SVP Product during Week 1 to introduce yourself
  • Attend your first Weekly Product Sync to see the team in action
  • Review previous QBR presentations to understand company-wide context
  • Schedule with your Product Director to understand your specific initiatives
  • Ask questions liberally - the cadence is designed to create alignment and learning
