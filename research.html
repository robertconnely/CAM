Healthcare Claims Terminology

Healthcare claims processing involves a specialized vocabulary. The Acronym & Terminology Glossary covers:

Key Acronyms

  • PI: Payment Integrity - Zelis product for claims accuracy and fraud detection
  • OON: Out-of-Network - Zelis product for managing unexpected high-cost care
  • RBP: Reference-Based Pricing - Benchmark-based pricing for OON claims
  • DRG: Diagnosis-Related Group - Hospital inpatient billing classification
  • CPT: Current Procedural Terminology - Physician procedure coding system
  • ICD: International Classification of Diseases - Diagnosis coding system
  • MCO: Managed Care Organization - Health insurance company
  • FWA: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse - Claims that are false, inefficient, or unnecessary
  • TPA: Third-Party Administrator - Company that administers claims for self-insured employers
  • EDI: Electronic Data Interchange - Standard format for claims transmission

Key Concepts

  • Pre-Payment Editing: Reviewing claims before payment to prevent overpayments
  • Post-Payment Recovery: Identifying overpayments after payment for recovery
  • Repricing: Reprocessing OON claims using benchmark rates
  • Clinical Validation: Using clinical rules to identify billing errors
  • Network Utilization: Percentage of claims paid to in-network providers
  • Savings Opportunity: Amount of potential cost reduction identified
  • Capture Rate: Percentage of identified savings actually realized
  • Medical Loss Ratio (MLR): Percentage of insurance premium spent on medical claims
