Radiology Center of Excellence

Vertical specialization initiative for medical imaging claims optimization.

Quick Summary

The Radiology Center of Excellence represents a vertical specialization opportunity within Payment Integrity. Medical imaging claims represent a high-value, high-complexity segment where specialized editing rules and clinical validation can drive differentiated savings. The initiative includes both organic capability building and inorganic M&A target evaluation.

Strategic Context

Market Opportunity

Medical imaging (radiology, CT, MRI, PET, ultrasound, nuclear medicine) represents one of the largest cost segments in healthcare claims processing:

  • Market Size: $20-25B annually in imaging-related claims
  • Growth Rate: 3-5% annually despite overutilization concerns
  • Complexity: High - imaging claims involve technical validation, clinical appropriateness, and specialized coding
  • Savings Potential: 2-5% of imaging claims contain errors or inappropriate utilization

Competitive Gaps

Current solutions in the market have limited imaging-specific capabilities:

  • Generic PI Tools: Lyric, Cotiviti use generic clinical rules that don't address imaging-specific issues
  • Imaging Workflow Vendors: Companies like RIS (Radiology Information Systems) vendors have workflow tools but not claims editing
  • Specialized Providers: Smaller imaging-focused companies lack scale and technology infrastructure

Zelis Competitive Advantage

  • Existing PI rules engine can be extended for imaging specialization
  • Payer relationships provide customer entry points for imaging COE
  • Data scale from existing customer base enables training specialized models
  • Technology platform can handle imaging-specific data and workflows

Radiology/Imaging Claims Overview

Claim Types & Complexity

  • Diagnostic Imaging: X-ray, ultrasound, CT, MRI, PET scans - highest volume
  • Interventional Radiology: Image-guided procedures with higher complexity and cost
  • Nuclear Medicine: Specialized imaging with unique coding and technical requirements
  • Cardiovascular Imaging: Echocardiography and other cardiac imaging

Common Imaging Billing Issues

  • Inappropriate Utilization: Imaging ordered without medical necessity or appropriate clinical context
  • Duplicate Studies: Multiple imaging studies of same anatomy within inappropriate time frames
  • Technical Component Errors: Incorrect billing of technical vs. professional components
  • Modifier Issues: Incorrect or missing modifiers affecting reimbursement
  • Coding Errors: Wrong CPT codes, incorrect anatomical site, wrong body laterality
  • Unbundling: Imaging procedures billed separately when they should be bundled
  • Contrast Agent Errors: Inappropriate use of contrast or incorrect billing

COE Capabilities (Build Plan)

Phase 1: Diagnostic Rules (Months 1-6)

Build specialized imaging rules library:

  • Appropriate use criteria (AUC) for imaging modalities
  • Clinical decision support for imaging necessity
  • Bundling and billing rules specific to imaging
  • Technical vs. professional component separation
  • Age and gender-appropriate utilization rules

Phase 2: Clinical Validation (Months 7-12)

Add clinical expertise to improve accuracy:

  • Radiologist-developed clinical appropriateness rules
  • Machine learning models for outcome prediction
  • Imaging pattern recognition for quality issues
  • Evidence-based guidelines integration

Phase 3: Specialized Tools (Months 13-18)

Build imaging-specific workflows and interfaces:

  • Image data integration where available
  • Radiology report NLP (Natural Language Processing)
  • Imaging ordering decision support
  • Provider feedback tools for imaging appropriateness

Financial Projections

Organic Build Approach

Internal development of imaging-specific rules and capabilities:

  • Investment: $2-3M over 18 months (product, engineering, clinical staff)
  • Timeline: First beta customers in Month 12, full launch Month 18
  • Customer Adoption: 5-10 customers in Year 1, ramp to 30+ by Year 3
  • Financial Impact: $1-2M ARR impact by Year 2, $5-7M by Year 3
  • IRR: 35-40% (attractive for Tier 2 initiative)

Inorganic Approach (M&A)

Acquisition of imaging-focused analytics or claims company:

  • Target Types: Imaging analytics vendor, radiology coding company, specialized PI vendor
  • Investment: $15-30M acquisition + integration (8-10x revenue for imaging-focused company)
  • Timeline: 6-month evaluation, 3-6 month integration
  • Synergies: Combine imaging expertise with Zelis platform, cross-sell to customer base
  • Financial Impact: $10-15M ARR from acquisition, $25-30M by Year 3 with platform integration

Implementation Strategy

Recommended Approach: Hybrid

Combination of organic build and targeted acquisition:

  • Year 1: Begin organic rules development internally while evaluating M&A targets
  • Year 2: If appropriate target identified, acquire and integrate. Otherwise, accelerate organic build
  • Year 3: Launch imaging COE product to all new customers, migrate interested existing customers

Success Metrics

  • Savings per Claim: Imaging-specific savings higher than generic PI tools
  • Customer Adoption: % of new customers choosing imaging COE add-on
  • Market Share: % of imaging claims market addressable by Zelis
  • Financial Performance: ARR growth and gross margin for imaging business
  • Clinical Outcomes: Reduction in inappropriate imaging utilization for customers

Risks & Mitigation

Market Risk

Risk: Imaging optimization might be lower priority than PI/OON

Mitigation: Position as add-on to existing PI solution, focus on high-savings opportunities

Competitive Risk

Risk: Larger competitors might develop similar capabilities

Mitigation: Move quickly to market, build clinical expertise moat, partner with radiologist advisors

Integration Risk (M&A)

Risk: Difficulty integrating acquired company into Zelis platform

Mitigation: Focus acquisition on technology fit, not just customer base. Plan phased integration.

Regulatory Risk

Risk: Regulatory changes around imaging appropriateness or prior authorization

Mitigation: Stay informed on regulatory trends, build flexibility into rules engine

Go-to-Market Strategy

Launch Customer Profile

  • Large national or regional payer (100K+ members minimum)
  • Current Zelis PI customer for cross-sell opportunity
  • Active imaging utilization management program
  • Willingness to be referenceable beta customer

Sales Approach

  • Primary Message: "Increase PI savings by 30-50% with imaging-specific rules"
  • Secondary Message: "Reduce inappropriate imaging utilization and member out-of-pocket costs"
  • Pricing: Premium add-on to PI pricing (1.5-2x base PI rate)
  • Partnership Approach: Partner with radiology societies and imaging quality organizations

Decision Framework

Organic vs. Inorganic Decision Criteria

The choice between organic build and M&A should be evaluated on:

  • Time to Market: M&A faster (12-18 months vs. 18-24 months)
  • Clinical Expertise: M&A brings established radiologist relationships
  • Technology Fit: Organic build better integrated with existing platform
  • Customer Base: M&A brings established customers for cross-sell
  • Cost: Organic build lower cost but longer payback period

Next Steps

To advance the Radiology COE initiative:

  1. Validate market opportunity with key customers (interviews with 3-5 payers)
  2. Identify potential M&A targets and evaluate fit
  3. Build detailed financial model for organic build approach
  4. Present to capital allocation forum for go/no-go decision
  5. If approved, initiate organizational setup and resource planning
